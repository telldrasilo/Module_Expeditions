# 2. Lore & world setting

## 2.1 Big picture

The world is **Kingdom of Arkedon** (working title): a medieval feudal state that recently survived a war of succession. Many lands are depopulated, baronial borders are blurred, and the wilds teem with monsters and anomalies. The Seekers’ Guild is a semi-official organization that takes contracts for scouting, rare materials, and eliminating threats.

Magic exists but is not ubiquitous or “epic.” Elements show up as natural yet dangerous forces: fire veins in volcanoes, miasma in swamps, time distortions in ancient ruins. There are no “chosen ones” or grand prophecies — only people trying to survive and earn a living.

## 2.2 Major historical layers

### 2.2.1 Ancient civilizations

- **Monolith Builders** — a long-vanished race or culture that left megalithic structures in mountains, forests, and deserts. Their works show perfect geometry and elemental resilience. Ruins may hold stone guardians, magnetism-themed artifacts, and bones.
- **Order of the Lightbearers** — a religious order that flourished centuries ago. They built beacon-temples on coasts, austere skerries in the tundra, and underground sanctuaries in crystal caves. Their legacy ties to **Light** and **Shadow** (as opposites).

### 2.2.2 Recent history

- **War of the Three Barons** (ended ~20 years ago) — a civil conflict that devastated central regions. Many castles lie in ruins; lands turned to forest or swamp. Remnant armies became bandit gangs; some commanders fled into the wilds.
- **Swamp Plague** — an unknown disease that struck eastern swamps ~10 years ago. Villages died out; the area grew more dangerous as rot-spawn multiplied.

## 2.3 Geography & key regions

The world has no rigid world map, but these **regions** (provinces) group adjacent biomes for coherence:

| Region | Biomes | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Eastern Marches** | `forest`, `swamp`, `bog` | Forested and marshy, bordering mountains. Many abandoned villages and watchtowers. |
| **Northern Reaches** | `tundra`, northern `mountains`, `deep_mines` | Harsh land with ore deposits and ancient mines. Sparse population: trappers and miners. |
| **Ashlands** | `volcanic`, part of `desert`, `crystal_caves` | Geothermally active: volcanoes, hot springs, crystal grottoes. |
| **Western Coast** | `ocean_coast`, `ancient_ruins`, `shadow_realm` (via portals) | Rocky shores, Lightbearer lighthouses, underwater caves, Shadow Realm exits. |
| **Central Baronies** | `forest`, `mountains` (foothills), southern `desert` | Most settled region; cities and the player’s workshop. |

## 2.4 Notable geographic features

- **Silverthread River** — a major river from the mountains through the Eastern Marches to the western ocean; trade routes and ruins along its banks.
- **Old Highroad** — an ancient road linking the Central Baronies to the Northern Reaches; partly abandoned but still used by caravans.
- **Stormspire Peak** — highest summit, perpetually storm-wrapped (ties to **Lightning**).
- **Black Mire** — heart of the swamps; rumored lair of an ancient beast that spawned the plague.
